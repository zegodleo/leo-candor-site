The Problem

In-house buying leaves money on the table.

Most shipping companies overpay on every deal — not by choice, but because they lack a trader's visibility into the real market.

01 / Market

No true market visibility

Your buyer sees a handful of quotes. They cannot tell if that is the real market price — or if you are overpaying on every deal.

02 / Quality

Quality taken on faith

Buyers rely on spec sheets alone. They cannot verify supplier reputation or track record. Substandard fuel can reach your vessels.

03 / Credit

Credit left on the table

Most shipping companies don't know how to negotiate credit lines with trading houses. Cash flow suffers unnecessarily.

04 / Claims

Claims are costly and slow

When issues arise — short delivery, quality disputes — in-house buyers lack the relationships to resolve them efficiently.

FAQ

What clients actually ask.

How do I know you're getting me the best price?

We share every supplier invoice on every deal. You can compare what you paid against any quote you receive independently. The saving is verifiable in black and white.

Are you tied to any particular supplier?

No. We have no supplier-side income, no commissions, no rebates, no spread. Our only revenue is your retainer and per-stem fee — so we have no incentive to prefer one supplier over another.

What if I already have my own supplier relationships?

That's fine. We can complement your existing relationships by adding a competitive layer, or replace them entirely. Many clients use us alongside their in-house buyer for benchmarking.

How quickly do I get quotes?

For standard ports (Singapore, Fujairah, Rotterdam) typically within 4–6 hours. For remote ports, 12–24 hours. Urgent enquiries handled out of hours.

Is there a lock-in contract?

No. Monthly retainer is rolling. Cancel any time with 30 days notice. We earn your business every month.

How do you handle quality disputes and short-delivery claims?

We've built relationships with all major suppliers over years of working the market. When a quality dispute or short-delivery claim arises, we drive the resolution on your behalf. Most claims close within two weeks.
